Exercise 4: Heating water

a) 1 calorie is the amount of energy needed to increase the temperature of 1g of water by 1 degree celcius. 1 calorie equals 4.184 joules. Make a calculator that converts a given amount of joules to calories and prints the result. Also, make a calculator that converts calories to joules.

b) The calorie is a very small unit, and although we in everyday speech indeed use the term "calories" when referring to the energy contents of food, the numbers we use and the numbers found on the back of food packaging, is that of kilocalories or kal. The relationship between a kcal, and a kjoule is the same as for calories and joules; 1 kcal = 4.184 kjoules. The relationship between kcal and water heating is also preserved, but upscaled; 1kcal heats 1000g of water by 1 degree celcius.

Write a program that can calculate how many joules are required to heat a full 10L bucket of water at sea level from 21 degrees celcius to boiling. Assume that the heat transfer is instantaneous, and that no heat is lost in the process.

c) Contrary to popular belief, animals of the genus camelus, such as camels and dromedarys, do not carry water in the "humps" on their backs. Although it is indeed true that they can drink and store large amounts of water, and survive for a long time without drinking, camels deal with the heat in much more interesting ways than one might expect. Two important adaptations in camels in order to deal with heat are a large body with concentrated fatty deposits in the "humps", and a high tolerance for changes in body temperature. The fat acts as a buffer that soaks thermal energy, causing the temperature rise in the body as a whole to be quite slow. During cold desert nights, the effect is reversed and the body is cooled more slowly. Allowing themselves to be heated reduced the thermal gradient against the environment, and thus the heating slows once the camel's body temperature rises.

Assume that a camel of 470kg receives a net heat gain averaging 90 \( \frac{joules}{second} \) throughout a day lasting 13.5 hours. Our camel consists of 55% water, and we will assume that the water is the only thing that affects body temperature. The camel starts out in the morning at a body temperature of 35.5 degrees celcius. Write a python program that calculates the final body temperature at the end of the day.

Hint. You can convert the amount of joules per second to a total amount of calories for the whole day in order to simplify your calculations.

d) The previous task was to design a program that models the body temperature of a camel given certain assumptions. Despite not being entirely realistic, how are models like these useful? How could we have improved the model to be more realistic?
